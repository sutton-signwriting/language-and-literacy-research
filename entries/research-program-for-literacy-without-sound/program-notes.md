# Research Program for Literacy Without Sound Program Notes

**Research Program for Literacy Without Sound**  
Study design, materials, corpora, and the next empirical steps for signed-language literacy

These notes record the practical logic behind the proposed research program.

## Why A Concrete Program Is Timely

Three conditions exist at the same time:

- the research argument is clearer than before
- the technical infrastructure is mature enough to support real study design
- Brazil provides the strongest currently documented empirical base

That combination makes a practical research-program document timely.

Without it, the work risks staying split between:

- high-level scientific argument
- scattered infrastructure concerns
- underused empirical evidence

## Core Design Principle

The core design principle is:

> build evidence for SignWriting rather than building a contest around other scripts

This does not forbid later comparison where useful.

It simply refuses to make comparison the center of the program.

That is strategically important because it keeps the agenda constructive and research-oriented.

## Why The Three Work Areas Fit Together

The three work areas are meant to reinforce each other.

| Work area | Main job | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Reading and literacy studies | study how SignWriting functions as durable text | turns the research framing into measurable text-use questions |
| Brazil-grounded empirical work | ground studies in real institutions, learners, and materials | prevents the program from floating at the level of pure theory |
| Corpus, materials, and reusable research infrastructure | build corpora, tasks, stimuli, and documentation | makes later studies cumulative rather than one-off |

The point is not to separate these permanently.

The point is to make sure each necessary area of work is visible.

## Early Studies That Are Realistic

The strongest early studies are those that:

- can be completed with modest resources
- produce reusable outputs
- sharpen later questions rather than overclaiming

That is why the first phase should emphasize:

- institutional mapping
- materials audit
- reader-experience work
- small reading-task pilots
- corpus-building and dataset design

These studies are realistic because they depend more on organization and partnership than on expensive instrumentation.

## Why Not Start With Neuroscience

Neuroscience remains promising.

It also remains expensive, slow to organize, and easy to overpromise.

The stronger route is:

1. identify real reader populations
2. collect and normalize usable materials
3. establish stable written stimuli and task designs
4. develop partnerships and ethics-ready protocols
5. then expand into larger behavioral or neural work if the earlier stages support it

This sequence is more disciplined and more likely to succeed.

## Why Brazil Is Central Rather Than Decorative

Brazil belongs inside the program design for practical reasons.

It contributes:

- real institutions
- real teachers
- real written materials
- real public history
- real evidence of curricular and literary use

Without that base, the program would risk feeling hypothetical.

With it, even modest studies become easier to justify.

The limit should be named just as clearly.

Brazil is the strongest place to begin, not the automatic proxy for every SignWriting community.

Findings from Libras materials, Brazilian institutions, and Brazilian reader histories will need replication in other signed languages, educational settings, and adoption contexts before they can support broad claims.

That is not a reason to avoid Brazil-centered work.

It is the scope rule that keeps Brazil-centered work scientifically useful.

## What Infrastructure Contributes To The Science

Infrastructure is not an afterthought to the science.

It contributes directly by making possible:

- text collection
- normalization and search
- annotation and metadata
- reproducible stimuli
- preservation and reuse
- later corpus and NLP work

This is one reason the research program should stay connected to *Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure and Program* even though its primary series is *Sutton SignWriting Language and Literacy Research*.

## What A Host Institution Would Make Easier

A credible academic setting would make several things easier at once:

- partnership and outreach
- ethics and study governance
- data stewardship
- grant coordination
- interdisciplinary framing across literacy, linguistics, computer science, and neuroscience

Those institutional needs connect directly to the host-structure and stewardship discussion in *Sutton SignWriting Infrastructure and Program*.

That does not mean the program cannot begin without a host institution.

It means the institution helps the program become stable and cumulative faster.

## Ethics And Governance Are Part Of The Design

The program needs ethics planning before it needs large claims.

That means:

- Deaf community involvement in study design
- accessible consent in signed and written forms where appropriate
- extra care for learners, minors, and classroom settings
- clear rights and reuse rules for texts and corpora
- local governance around data sharing and public release
- results returned in forms useful to participating communities

An institutional ethics board is necessary for human-subjects work, but it should not be treated as the whole ethical structure.

For this program, ethics is part of research quality.

## Good-Idea Test

This program is a good idea if it remains:

- SignWriting-centered
- empirically grounded
- focused enough to begin
- infrastructure-aware
- reusable in its outputs

It becomes a weaker idea if it drifts into:

- comparison for its own sake
- abstract theory without studies
- oversized promises before materials and partnerships exist

## Bottom Line

The most useful next move is not a bigger theory.

It is a concrete SignWriting-centered research program that:

- starts with real populations and materials
- uses Brazil as the strongest documented base
- treats infrastructure as part of the science
- builds toward later literacy, corpus, cognition, and education work in a disciplined way
